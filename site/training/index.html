Why training

When teams are buried in delivery work, they rarely have time to step back and align on prioritization, product discovery, and other core product management practices. Even when strong product leaders exist internally, they rarely have weekly capacity to train teams consistently. Coaching-based training provides dedicated time and structure so teams move beyong just executing requests: they build products that delight customers and deliver business results.

Training overview

I deliver highly tailored training for product teams, along with targeted coaching and office hours to strengthen learning and alignment.

This program helps organizations set cross-team product management standards. It creates space to rise above the day-to-day, deepen understanding of the problem, customer impact, and business impact, and make better decisions without adding process.

Each package is shaped to the group's needs. I partner with the Group Lead to understand the team's current challenges and goals, then design the training accordingly.

  • Monthly small group training
  • Weekly office hours for real-time coaching
  • Attendees present how they applied what they learned
  • Curriculum tailored to your team's goals and challenges
  • Skills and a starting framework to build your own product practices (not a rigid system)

3+1 program structure (example)

Every team's needs are different. This is just an example of a potential training course.

Each session builds on the last to develop shared product judgment.

  1. What is PM: Align on what product management is (vs. product ownership) and practical first steps.
  2. Product discovery: A starting framework (not a prescriptive system) with 15 structured questions and strong vs. weak examples to assess opportunities, recommend go/no-go, and return with clear context.
  3. Prioritization: A starting framework (not a prescriptive system) with 9 scoring categories for consistent value/effort, plus how to draw the waterline when initiatives exceed bandwidth; applies strategy and supports clearer internal and external conversations.
  4. Recap and application presentation: PMs show how they applied what they learned.
